Chapter 2

   
HOLD Onto Hope - When Your Professional World Falls Apart
       

Hope is not passive waiting—it's active faith that refuses to let circumstances write the final chapter of your story.

       
________________________________________
       

"Will we have anything to eat? Will we have anything to drink? Will we have any clothes to wear?"

       

These aren't just ancient worries from Matthew's gospel. These are the 3 AM thoughts of every professional who's just been laid off, every entrepreneur whose funding fell through, every creative whose income stream just dried up.

       

"How will I pay the mortgage?"
    "What about health insurance?"
    "How do I tell my family?"
    "What if I never find work again?"
    "Am I too old to start over?"
    "Did I waste all those years building something that's now gone?"

       

If you're in professional crisis right now, these questions are probably cycling through your mind on repeat. The worry feels logical, practical, even responsible. After all, someone has to figure out how you're going to survive this, right?

       

Wrong.

       

Well, partially wrong. You absolutely need to take practical action. But worry is not action - it's the enemy of the hope you need to navigate this crisis with your faith intact.

       
________________________________________
       

What Hope Actually Means for Professionals

       

Hope is not positive thinking. Hope is not pretending everything is fine when your career is in shambles. Hope is not spiritual bypassing that ignores the practical realities of bills and responsibilities.

       

Biblical hope is confident expectation based on God's proven character and promises.

       

For professionals in crisis, this means:

       
           
  • Hope believes that God has a plan even when your five-year plan is destroyed
    •        
  • Hope acts on opportunities while trusting God for the outcome
    •        
  • Hope perseveres through the valley because it knows there's a mountain on the other side
    •        
  • Hope works while depending on divine provision
    •        
  • Hope rests in God's timing while actively participating in His plan
    •    
       

This isn't passive waiting. This is active faith that keeps moving forward even when you can't see the full path.

       
________________________________________
       

The Professional's Guide to Worry-Free Faith

       

After you've asked for what you want, stop worrying, have faith. No worries on the faith journey does not mean that everything will be just perfect. It means that because you have trust in God, everything will be all right.

       

Let me be clear: "everything will be all right" doesn't mean you'll get your old job back or your failed business will resurrect. It means that God will provide what you need, when you need it, in ways you probably can't imagine right now.

       

The Matthew 6 Survival Guide for Professional Crisis

       
       

"I tell you not to worry about your life. Don't worry about having something to eat, drink, or wear. Isn't life more than food or clothing?"

       

Professional Translation: Don't let survival fears drive your career decisions. You are more than your job title, your income level, or your professional status.

   
       
       

"Look at the birds in the sky! They don't plant or harvest. They don't even store grain in barns. Yet your Father in heaven takes care of them. Aren't you worth more than birds?"

       

Professional Application: Even birds have to work - they hunt, they build nests, they care for their young. But they don't worry about tomorrow's worm while they're hunting today's. They do their part and trust the ecosystem to provide. You can work diligently while trusting God's provision.

   
       
       

"Can worry make you live longer?"

       

Career Reality Check: Can worry get you hired faster? Can anxiety improve your interview performance? Can fear enhance your creativity? Can stress make your networking more effective? Worry is not only useless - it's counterproductive to your professional recovery.

    2.

"Your Father in heaven knows that you need all of these. But more than anything else, put God's work first and do what he wants. Then the other things will be yours as well."

       

This verse has revolutionized how I approach professional challenges. It's not about choosing God over practical concerns - it's about aligning your professional path with God's purposes and watching Him take care of the details you can't control.

       

In practical terms:

       
           
  • Integrity over Income: Choose the path that honors God, even if it pays less initially
    •        
  • Purpose over Position: Seek roles that utilize your divine gifts, not just your professional skills
    •        
  • Service over Success: Ask how you can serve others through your work, not just how you can advance
    •        
  • Trust over Control: Make wise decisions and release the outcomes to God
    •    
       

This isn't naive idealism. This is the most practical career strategy you can employ because it aligns you with unlimited resources.

When Jesus talked about moving mountains, He wasn't speaking metaphorically about small obstacles. He was talking about the impossible, immovable, this-will-never-change realities that block your path forward.

       

In professional terms, mountains look like:

       
           
  • "You'll never get hired at your age"
    •        
  • "This industry is dead - there's no future here"
    •        
  • "You don't have the right connections"

  • "Your background doesn't qualify you"

  • "The economy is too bad for new businesses"

  • "Creative work doesn't pay the bills"

These aren't just negative thoughts - they're mountain-sized obstacles that seem immovable, permanent, and completely beyond your power to change.

       

But here's what I've learned through decades of professional crises: Faith doesn't just help you climb mountains - it teaches you how to move them entirely.

       

Let me show you how this works with three real-life examples from my own journey. These aren't sanitized success stories - they're raw, messy, impossible situations where faith literally made a way out of no way.

Mountain #1: The Bureaucratic Impossibility

   

Faith vs. The System That Can't Be Beat

       

Picture this: You're a young mother with three little boys, barely making ends meet, and your car registration is a nightmare of unpaid parking tickets compounded by new laws that tripled the penalties. You need this car to get to work and transport your children, but the system says you owe thousands of dollars you don't have.

       

This was my DMV mountain.

       

The penalties were exorbitant - like three times the amount of the original tickets due to new legislation. I had created what I honestly called "a holy mess" of the situation. But here's what I learned about faith in impossible bureaucratic situations:

       

Sometimes God works through systems, not around them.

       

I went into my bedroom, got down on my knees, and asked God, in the name of Jesus, to take care of this matter for me. I asked for forgiveness for creating such a mess, and then I had the strongest urge to get to the DMV immediately.

       
       

The Professional Lesson:

       

When you've exhausted your own resources, prayer isn't your last resort - it should be your first strategy.

   
       

I raced over there and parked right in front (miracle #1 - the DMV in California is always crowded). I walked in to find the place nearly empty (miracle #2). I approached a woman behind the counter named Sue Anne, who pulled up my record.

       

"You owe $2.00, Ma'am."

       

I asked her to check again. She did.

       

"You owe $2.00."

       

I believe God wiped it all out. I paid $2.00 and SHOUTED in the parking lot.

       
       

The Professional Application:

       

When facing bureaucratic mountains in your career:

       
               
  • Government agencies that seem determined to block your business license
    •            
  • Professional boards that question your qualifications
    •            
  • Legal systems that seem stacked against your case
    •            
  • Corporate bureaucracies that lose your paperwork repeatedly
    •            
  • Insurance companies that deny legitimate claims
    •        
       

Faith Strategy: Pray specifically, act immediately when prompted, and expect supernatural intervention in natural systems.

Mountain #2: The Impossible Housing Market

   

Faith vs. Financial Limitations and Market Forces

       

After my mother was diagnosed with terminal cancer, we needed a house big enough for six people. We found one that seemed perfect and started the buying process. Then everything that could go wrong did:

       
           
  • The house was overpriced with multiple bidders competing
    •        
  • Real estate agents were crooked and gave it to the highest bidder
    •        
  • We were outbid despite having solid credit and qualifications
    •        
  • Deal fell through right before closing
    •    
       

Meanwhile, my mother's belongings were already being shipped from DC, my grandmother was dying in Tennessee, and we were literally homeless with nowhere to put a terminally ill family member after being outbid on what seemed like our perfect house.

       

This was my housing mountain.

       

After the house deal fell through, my mother and I came home and did what Black women do when we're in a mess: we cleaned and folded clothes. But later, I quietly disappeared into my bedroom, closed the door, got on my knees, and asked God for help.

       

Two days later, we learned about a house in the San Fernando Valley - an area we'd never even considered.

       
       

The Professional Lesson:

       

Sometimes God's plan requires you to expand your geographical or industry boundaries.

   
       

When I walked into that house and saw the hardwood floors, I gasped. My old house was built in 1947, this new house was built in 2000, yet they had the exact same engraved design in the wooden floors - a design I'd only ever seen in my old house.

       

I heard God whisper: "This is it, the house that I prepared for you all."

       

More confirmations followed:

       
           
  • The loan company had the same name as a childhood friend
    •        
  • The street name was the same as a street I grew up on
    •        
  • The house was much less expensive than the first one, but appraised for much more than what we paid
    •        
  • The seller left all the furniture
    •        
  • Escrow closed in three weeks
    •    
       
       

The Professional Application:

       

When facing resource mountains in your career:

       
               
  • Competitive bidding situations where you're outbid
    •            
  • Crooked agents or unethical business practices
    •            
  • Locations that are out of your price range
    •            
  • Equipment or tools you can't afford
    •            
  • Market conditions that seem to work against you
    •            
  • Timing that appears all wrong
    •        
       

Faith Strategy: Ask specifically, stay open to unexpected solutions, and watch for confirmations that you're on the right path.

   
       
________________________________________
       

Mountain #3: The Missing Person Crisis

   

Faith vs. The Worst-Case Professional Nightmare

       

My oldest son, who is autistic, decided to prove his independence by taking a bus across town without money or a cell phone to get back. When he didn't return after several hours, we called the police. Because of his disability and medication needs, it became a critical missing person case.

       

This was my family emergency mountain - the kind that destroys professional focus and derails every career plan.

       

Investigators swarmed my house. A helicopter circled overhead. My son's picture was circulated on the internet. It was January, bitterly cold, and as night fell with no sign of him, I thought I would literally lose my mind.

       
       

The Professional Reality:

       

Sometimes personal crises become professional mountains because they completely overwhelm your ability to function in work situations.

   
       

On the second day, realizing I needed help beyond human resources, I got down on my knees and asked God to give me the scripture to bring him home. I opened my Bible and found Psalm 91:

       

"He who dwells in the secret place of the Most High shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty... He shall cover you with His feathers, and under His wings you shall take refuge... A thousand may fall at your side, and ten thousand at your right hand; but it shall not come near you."

       

After reading that scripture, I knew my son would return home safely. Calm and peace came over me even though he was missing for two nights and three days.

       

On the third day, a friend printed hundreds of flyers and distributed them in Santa Monica. Police told her staff they couldn't post them, so they let the flyers blow in the wind, hoping someone who had seen him would pick one up.

       

One flyer blew to the feet of a street performer who looked up and saw my son standing right there.

       

The performer called my cell phone number from the flyer, and the miracle unfolded exactly as I knew it would.

       

When I got him home, my son told me he'd slept on a bench with homeless people for two nights, but he said he had angels sitting on his shoulders - his grandmothers, Nanny and Madear - keeping him safe. He said, "I was not afraid. They [homeless people] were really nice to me."

       
       

The Professional Application:

       

When personal mountains threaten your professional life:

       
               
  • Family medical emergencies that require extended leave
    •            
  • Mental health crises that affect work performance
    •            
  • Financial disasters that destroy focus and stability
    •            
  • Legal issues that demand time and emotional energy
    •            
  • Relationship breakdowns that impact every area of life
    •        
       

Faith Strategy: Recognize that personal mountains often have professional implications, but God's protection covers every area of your life when you trust Him completely.

The Mountain-Moving Formula for Professionals

       

Based on these real-life experiences, here's the practical formula for overcoming any professional crisis:

       

Step 1: Name Your Mountain

   

Be specific. Don't pray "help me with work stuff." Pray "God, I need this specific position" or "I need this exact amount of funding by this date" or "I need breakthrough in this particular relationship."

       

Step 2: Acknowledge Your Limitations

   

Admit you're powerless. The DMV system was bigger than me. The housing market was beyond my control. My son's safety was out of my hands. Recognition of your limitations is the beginning of accessing unlimited divine resources.

       

Step 3: Ask with Authority

   

Pray in Jesus' name. This isn't magic words - it's accessing the authority of someone who has already overcome every mountain you'll ever face.

       

Step 4: Act on Promptings

   

Move when prompted. I felt urged to go to the DMV immediately. I felt led to look in a different area for housing. I felt directed to specific scriptures. Faith without corresponding action is dead.

       

Step 5: Expect the Supernatural

   

Look for God's fingerprints. $2.00 instead of thousands. Same floor pattern in different houses. Flyers blowing to exactly the right person. God loves to leave His signature on the solution.

       

Step 6: Give God the Glory

   

Share the story. These aren't coincidences or luck. They're demonstrations of God's power available to anyone with faith. Your testimony becomes someone else's hope.
